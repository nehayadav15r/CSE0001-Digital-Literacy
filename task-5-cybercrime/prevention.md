# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: A Guide for Indian College Students

As digital transactions and online learning become the norm, college students are increasingly targeted by cybercriminals. Use this checklist to audit your digital habits and secure your personal data.

---

## 📋 Security Checklist

### 💳 UPI & Financial Safety (High Priority)
- [ ] **1. PIN is for Sending, Not Receiving:** Remember that entering your UPI PIN is only required to **send** money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive a cashback" or "claim a refund," it is a scam.
- [ ] **2. Verify the 'Verified' Name:** Before hitting 'Pay' on apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, or Paytm, look at the actual name registered to the VPA (Virtual Payment Address). Scammers often use logos of famous brands as their profile pictures to deceive you.
- [ ] **3. Set Daily Transaction Limits:** Use your bank's mobile app to set a reasonable daily limit on UPI and ATM transactions to minimize potential loss if your account is compromised.

### 📱 Account & Device Security
- [ ] **4. The 2FA Rule:** Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your University Portal, Gmail, and WhatsApp. Use an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator) rather than SMS-based OTPs where possible.
- [ ] **5. Avoid 'Free' Public Wi-Fi:** Never log into your bank account or student dashboard using campus cafe or railway station Wi-Fi. Use your mobile hotspot or a reputable VPN service.
- [ ] **6. Audit Third-Party App Access:** Periodically check your Google/Facebook settings to see which "Quiz" or "Photo Editing" apps still have access to your data and revoke permissions for those you no longer use.

### 🕵️ Social Engineering Awareness
- [ ] **7. Beware of Fake Internship/Job Offers:** If you receive a "Work from Home" offer on WhatsApp or Telegram asking for a registration fee or asking you to "like YouTube videos" for money, block the sender immediately.
- [ ] **8. Reverse Image Search:** If you are approached by a stranger on Instagram or LinkedIn, use a reverse image search on their profile picture to ensure they aren't using a stolen identity.

---

## 🚨 What to do if you are scammed?

The first **2 hours** after a financial fraud are known as the **"Golden Hour."** Reporting the crime within this window significantly increases the chances of the bank freezing the stolen funds before the criminal can withdraw them.



### Official Reporting Channels:
1.  **Call the National Helpline:** Dial **1930** immediately. This is a 24/7 dedicated line for reporting financial cyber fraud.
2.  **File an Online Complaint:** Visit the **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal** at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in).
3.  **Notify Your Bank:** Use the official "Report Fraud" button inside your banking app to block your cards and accounts.

---

> **Pro-Tip:** Save the number **1930** in your contacts as "Cyber Crime Helpline" so you don't have to search for it during an emergency.
